## Concepts Introduced

- Workflow steps are sequential by default; contiguous steps sharing a
  workflow-local `parallel_with` label form one parallel stage.
- Stage indexes are assigned at submission. Only stage 0 is initially queued;
  successful stage completion compiles the next stage JIT.
- Normal step output is a typed object, fan-out output is a list, and parallel
  fan-out output may be a list of lists.
- Step output references use explicit `workflow.step[index]`; a `previous`
  convenience alias is deferred.
- The database is the sole queue authority, with separate queued, running,
  completed, and failed placement tables and transactional insert/delete state
  transitions.
- `run_id` and `stage_index` follow work through placement tables;
  `attempt_id` is unique per worker claim and changes on retry.
- Workflow/configuration snapshots use schema-versioned text JSON, while
  scheduling identities and state remain relational.
- Attempt liveness uses worker heartbeats, leases, caretaker reconciliation,
  and fencing tokens.
- Reuse follows a convergence model: controller-calculated execution identity
  plus worker-calculated `pre_state_hash` and `post_state_hash` over a
  plugin-defined observable state.
